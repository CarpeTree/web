Structural Pruning

Structural pruning shapes the framework of a young or mature tree to ensure it grows with a sturdy, balanced form. Our tree care specialists selectively remove or shorten weak, crossing, or competing limbs to encourage one well-defined central leader and strong branch structure. Techniques like crown cleaning (removing dead or weak wood), crown raising (lifting low limbs for clearance), and tipping (reducing leverage on overreaching branches) are employed as needed to meet your tree's specific needs. We follow industry-recognized ANSI A300 pruning standards – meaning no "topping" or other harmful cuts that could injure the tree.

Why is structural pruning important?

  • Stronger, Safer Trees: Proactive pruning prevents weak branch unions, reducing storm damage risk.
  • Healthier Growth: Improved sunlight and air circulation boost tree vigor and reduce pest/disease issues.
  • Reduced Future Costs: Early pruning minimizes the need for costly emergency removals later.

Local Considerations:

The Kootenay's variable climate, with its mild, wet winters and dry summers, climate leads to vigorous tree growth. Without structural pruning, trees may become overgrown or hazardous. For example, codominant stems (two main trunks of equal size) are common in certain conifers and are structurally weaker due to frequently included bark, making them prone to splitting. Our science-backed approach includes techniques such as subordination pruning – gently reducing a co-dominant stem so that one leader dominates – to prevent splits in the canopy.

Fruit Tree Pruning

Fruit trees require a specialized pruning approach to maximize fruit yield while maintaining tree health. We prune fruit trees (such as walnut, apple, pear, plum, and cherry trees common in the kootenays) with a focus on creating an open, balanced canopy that allows sunlight to reach fruit-bearing wood. By thinning out dense clusters of branches and removing vertical "water sprouts," we help sunlight and air circulate, which improves fruit quality and reduces fungal diseases (a critical concern in our damp West Kootenays climate).

Our arborists are knowledgeable about proper fruit tree pruning techniques:

  • Eliminating dead, diseased, or crossing branches to prevent pest spread and rubbing damage.
  • Removing vigorous upright shoots (suckers and water sprouts) to direct the tree's energy into fruit production.
  • Thinning the canopy to enhance air circulation, which reduces disease pressure and promotes better fruit development.

Soil & Root Health (Air Spading & Mulching)

Healthy roots are the foundation of a healthy tree. In urban and suburban environments, soil compaction, nutrient deficiencies, and poor drainage can all stress your trees' root systems. Our Soil & Root Health services address these issues using modern, tree-friendly techniques that improve the underground conditions without harming the tree.

One of our flagship services is air spading – a process of using a high-powered air tool to break up compacted soil around the root zone. This technique safely loosens soil without damaging roots, allowing better water infiltration and root respiration. We often follow up air spading with organic amendments like compost, biochar, and mycorrhizal fungi to boost soil health.

We also offer guidance on proper mulching and soil management: applying wood chip mulch over the root zone to conserve moisture, moderate soil temperature, and add organic matter as it breaks down.

Client Education – Soil & Root Care in the koots:

  • Signs of Soil Problems: Slow growth, wilting in summer despite watering, or dieback in the crown often signal poor soil conditions.
  • Air Spade Benefits: Air spading can save a struggling tree by relieving compaction, improving aeration, and encouraging root expansion.
  • Mulching and Aftercare: Proper mulching reduces weed growth, improves soil structure, and insulates roots from extreme temperatures.
