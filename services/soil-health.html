Soil & Root Health Services

Strengthening trees from the ground up.

Overview

Healthy roots are the foundation of a healthy tree. In urban and suburban environments, soil compaction, nutrient deficiencies, and poor drainage can all stress your trees' root systems. Our Soil & Root Health services address these issues using modern, tree-friendly techniques that improve the underground conditions without harming the tree.

West Kootenays Soil Considerations

The West Kootenays region presents unique soil challenges, from clay-heavy soils that compact easily to areas with thin topsoil over rocky substrates. Our variable climate, with wet winters and dry summers, can further stress root systems. Our soil health approaches are tailored to these local conditions, ensuring your trees develop robust root systems that can withstand our regional challenges.

Professional Mulching

We also offer guidance on proper mulching and soil management: applying wood chip mulch over the root zone to conserve moisture, moderate soil temperature, and add organic matter as it breaks down.

  • Proper Application: Correct depth and distribution to avoid stem damage
  • Material Selection: Choosing the right mulch type for your specific trees
  • Mulch Maintenance: Refreshing and managing existing mulch layers
  • Root Protection: Creating buffer zones to prevent mechanical damage

Signs of Soil Problems

Being able to identify soil issues early can save your trees from decline. Watch for these warning signs:

  • Slow Growth: Stunted or minimal new growth despite adequate sunlight
  • Wilting Despite Watering: Trees that show drought stress even with regular irrigation
  • Crown Dieback: Progressive dying of branches from the tips inward
  • Soil Crusting: Hard surface layer that water cannot penetrate
  • Standing Water: Puddles that remain long after rainfall
