Cat, Drone & Raptor Rescue

Professional rescue services for animals and objects stuck in trees.

Overview

When pets, wildlife, or valuable equipment become trapped in trees, our specialized rescue team combines arboricultural expertise with animal handling skills to ensure safe retrieval. Our certified arborists have the climbing skills, equipment, and experience to safely access even the most challenging canopy locations.

We understand that these situations can be stressful for both animals and their owners. Our approach prioritizes the safety and well-being of the animal while minimizing additional stress during the rescue process.

Cat Rescue

Cats are natural climbers but sometimes get stuck when they climb too high or become frightened. Our gentle approach minimizes stress while ensuring their safe return.

  • Specialized cat handling techniques
  • Gentle capture methods
  • Priority scheduling for urgent situations

Drone Recovery

When expensive drones become entangled in branches, our team can safely retrieve them without causing further damage to the equipment or the tree.

  • Careful extraction techniques
  • Equipment protection protocols
  • Experience with various drone models

Raptor & Wildlife Rescue

For injured birds of prey or other wildlife trapped in trees, we work with licensed wildlife rehabilitators to ensure proper handling and care.

  • Wildlife handling training
  • Coordination with rehabilitation experts
  • Safe containment methods

Our Rescue Approach

  • Animal Rescue Training: Specialized techniques for safely handling stressed animals
  • Careful Approach: Minimizing additional stress through gentle handling methods
  • Appropriate Equipment: Specialized gear for different rescue scenarios
  • Rapid Response: Priority scheduling for urgent animal situations
  • Wildlife Coordination: Connections with licensed wildlife rehabilitators when needed

Each rescue situation is unique, and we adapt our techniques accordingly. Our team has successfully rescued cats from heights exceeding 100 feet, retrieved drones from dense conifer canopies, and assisted with the recovery of injured raptors for rehabilitation.
