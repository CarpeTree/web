Tree Removal & Alternatives

Thoughtful, professional tree removal with ecological alternatives when appropriate.

While we prioritize tree preservation, there are legitimate circumstances where removal becomes necessary. Our team provides professional, safe removal services with a focus on exploring alternatives whenever possible. From complete removals to creating wildlife habitats and managing fire risks, we offer comprehensive solutions tailored to your specific circumstances.

Tree Removal

  • Technical Precision: Advanced rigging and directional felling techniques
  • Safety Focus: Comprehensive hazard assessment and site protection measures
  • Equipment Options: Choosing the right approach from climbing to crane removals
  • Complete Cleanup: Thorough debris management and site restoration
  • Stump Solutions: Grinding, removal, or treatment options to suit your needs

Wildlife Snags (Habitat Trees)

  • Ecological Assessment: Evaluation of habitat potential and wildlife value
  • Risk Reduction: Removing hazardous portions while preserving habitat
  • Feature Creation: Adding cavities, platforms, and other wildlife elements
  • Aesthetic Considerations: Maintaining visual appeal while enhancing habitat
  • Educational Resources: Information on wildlife species that benefit from your habitat tree

Fire-Safe Tree Management

  • Defensible Space Creation: Strategic vegetation management around structures
  • Fuel Load Reduction: Removing fire-prone materials and ladder fuels
  • Species Management: Advice on fire-resistant plantings and spacing
  • Selective Removal: Targeting high-risk specimens while preserving healthy ones
  • Compliance Assistance: Meeting local regulations and insurance requirements

When Removal Is Necessary

  • Structural Compromise: Trees with severe defects that cannot be mitigated
  • Disease Management: Controlling spread of serious pathogens
  • Construction Conflicts: Unavoidable impacts from development
  • Extreme Root Damage: Trees with compromised stability
  • Risk Thresholds: When mitigation cannot reduce risk to acceptable levels
