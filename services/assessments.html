Tree Risk Assessments & Consultations

Science-backed tree health evaluations conducted by ISA Certified Arborists.

Our risk assessment services employ the latest arboricultural research and techniques to evaluate the structural integrity, health, and potential hazards associated with your trees. Using a methodical approach aligned with industry standards (ISA TRAQ protocols), we provide detailed reports that inform thoughtful, proactive tree care decisions.

Assessment Services

  • Basic Visual Assessments: Comprehensive visual inspection of tree health and structure
  • Level 2 Assessments: Detailed evaluation with specialized tools including mallet soundings, moisture meters, and resistance drilling
  • Pre-Development Surveys: Complete property tree inventory with health ratings and preservation recommendations
  • Construction Impact Analysis: Evaluation of potential impacts from building activities with protection plans
  • Hazard Tree Evaluation: Identification of structural weaknesses and failure potential with risk mitigation options

Consultation Types

  • Tree Selection & Planting: Expert guidance on species choice and installation best practices
  • Property Management Plans: Long-term strategies for tree health and succession planning
  • Tree Preservation: Solutions for maintaining trees during landscape changes
  • Health Recovery Programs: Custom treatment plans for declining trees
  • View Enhancement: Ethical approaches to improve views while preserving tree health

Our Assessment Process

  • Client Consultation: Understanding your specific concerns and objectives
  • Site Evaluation: Thorough examination of trees and surrounding environment
  • Data Collection: Measurements, observations, and technical readings
  • Analysis: Professional interpretation of collected information
  • Detailed Reporting: Clear documentation with findings and recommendations

Client Education

  • Risk Explanation: Clear communication about identified issues
  • Treatment Options: Comprehensive overview of available interventions
  • Maintenance Guidance: Long-term care recommendations
  • Priority Planning: Help with scheduling time-sensitive treatments
  • Ongoing Support: Resources for continued tree health
